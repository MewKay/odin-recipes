Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish that typically consists of layered sheets of wide flat pasta, a tomato-based sauce, and various other ingredients such as ground meat, cheese, and vegetables. The layers are typically topped with a béchamel sauce and baked in the oven until the cheese on top is melted and bubbly.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Tomato sauce
  • Ground meat (beef, pork, or sausage) or vegetables for a vegetarian version
  • Cheese (usually ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan)
  • Béchamel sauce
  • Herbs and spices (such as basil, oregano, and garlic)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions, then drain and rinse them under cold water.
  3. In a large skillet over medium-high heat, cook the ground meat until browned. If using vegetables, sauté them until tender.
  4. Add tomato sauce to the skillet and season with salt, pepper, and herbs to taste. Simmer the sauce for 10-15 minutes until it thickens.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix together the ricotta cheese with an egg, salt, pepper, and herbs.
  6. Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish with cooking spray, then spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on the bottom.
  7. Lay a single layer of cooked lasagna noodles over the sauce, then spread a layer of the ricotta mixture on top.
  8. Add another layer of tomato sauce, followed by a layer of mozzarella cheese and Parmesan cheese.
  9. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used up, making sure to end with a layer of cheese on top.
  10. Pour the béchamel sauce over the top layer of cheese.
  11. Cover the dish with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
  12. Remove the foil and bake for another 25-30 minutes, or until the cheese on top is golden brown and bubbly.
  13. Let the lasagna cool for 10-15 minutes before serving.
