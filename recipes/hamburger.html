Hamburger

Description

A hamburger is a sandwich made of a cooked ground beef patty served between two slices of bread, often with toppings such as lettuce, tomato, cheese, and condiments.

Ingredients

  • Hamburger buns
  • Lettuce
  • Ground beef
  • Tomato
  • Onion
  • Pickles
  • Cheese
  • Ketchup

Steps

  1. Preheat a grill or stovetop pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Form the ground beef into patties that are slightly larger than the diameter of the hamburger buns.
  3. Season the patties with salt and pepper on both sides.
  4. Grill or pan-fry the patties for 3-5 minutes per side, or until cooked to your desired doneness.
  5. While the patties are cooking, slice the tomato and onion into thin rounds.
  6. Toast the hamburger buns on the grill or in the pan for a minute or two, until lightly browned.
  7. Assemble the burgers by placing the cooked patties on the bottom halves of the buns.
  8. Add a slice of cheese on top of each patty and let it melt for a minute.
  9. Add a few slices of tomato, onion, and pickles on top of the cheese.
  10. Squeeze ketchup on top of the veggies and cover with the top half of the bun.
